Origins and Development

The concept for Pokémon Go originated from an April Fools’ Day collaboration between Google and The Pokémon Company in 2014, known as the “Google Maps: Pokémon Challenge.” This initiative, conceived by Tsunekazu Ishihara and Satoru Iwata, envisioned integrating Pokémon into real-world maps. Ishihara, impressed by Niantic’s earlier AR game, Ingress, saw potential in combining its location-based gameplay with the Pokémon universe. Consequently, Niantic, then a subsidiary of Google, utilized data from Ingress to populate PokéStops and Gyms in Pokémon Go. The game was officially announced in September 2015, with Tatsuo Nomura serving as Director and Product Manager, and Junichi Masuda, a longtime composer for the Pokémon series, contributing to the soundtrack and design.

Launch and Initial Success

Upon its release in July 2016, Pokémon Go rapidly became a global phenomenon. By the end of that month, it had generated over $160 million in revenue, with daily earnings surpassing those of established titles like Clash of Clans and Candy Crush. The game’s average daily usage on Android devices exceeded that of Snapchat, Tinder, Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook. Within 90 days, Pokémon Go reached $600 million in revenue, setting a record as the fastest mobile game to achieve this milestone.
